In the first semester at SFU, I mainly focused on a project in the Big Data Lab called WikiTrends. It is a web-based application that allows users to enter keywords and time frames they are interested in, and it will display the trends of these keywords in the given period.

We use the datasets of Wikipedia in this project.

The motivation for us to build this project is to break the information cocoons: when using social media such as Reddit and Instagram, the posts recommended to my accounts are mainly based on what I read previously, which means that it is rarely related to what happens in the recent time or focused by a large number of people. Even though Twitter has the function to see the trends, it is located based - I can only see the Canadian trends now. Since Wikipedia is the primary online encyclopedia all over the world, it is appropriate to connect the number of views of Wiki pages with peopleâ€™s interests. To assist our users in knowing better about the overall trends of their languages (since seeing some unknown letters/characters is not user-friendly), we provide the function showing the Top 10 Wiki Pages viewed by certain days.


Fig.1. Daily Top 10 Wiki Pages viewed on 01/07/2023

In this project, I mainly contributed to the frontend setting and connecting it with the backend. We chose to use React as the front end since the component-based architecture is convenient to reuse, and various JS libraries support different functions.
